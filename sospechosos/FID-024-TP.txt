This article explores the benefits and risks of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in education in relation to fundamental human rights. It is based on an EU scoping study [Berendt, B., A. Littlejohn, P. Kern, P. Mitros, X. Shacklock, and M. Blakemore. 2017. Big Data for Monitoring Educational Systems. Luxembourg: Publications Office of the European Union. https://publications.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/94cb5fc8-473e-11e7-aea8-01aa75ed71a1/]. The study considers the potential of AI and 'Big Data' to enhance real-time monitoring of the education system, while also addressing the implications for the fundamental human rights and freedoms of both teachers and learners. The analysis emphasizes the necessity of balancing the benefits and risks as AI tools are developed, marketed, and implemented. The article concludes with a call to integrate considerations of the benefits and risks of AI in education into the development, marketing, and deployment processes of these tools. It raises questions about which body or organization should be responsible for regulating AI in education, especially since AI affects not only data protection and privacy but also fundamental rights in general.
