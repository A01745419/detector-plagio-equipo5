The educational applications of AI embody a fusion of what Pasteur's Quadrant delineates as use-inspired basic and pure applied research. This article offers a comprehensive survey of both classical and burgeoning architectures for AI implementation in education. While early pioneers concentrated on devising personalized teaching systems tailored to individual learners, contemporary endeavors have broadened their scope to encompass collaborative learning environments and contextual factors. Throughout this evolutionary journey, diverse Grand Challenges have emerged, underscoring the persistent hurdles confronting AI in the realm of education.
